The graphic summary from my Weatherpy depicts five graphs. The world-like graph shows all places where measurements were reported for the analysis. Note that there are no measurements in the oceans, and very few in the deserts of the world. There are also few in much of China and Russia. I adopted the convention of displaying latitude in the oriention that is spacially oriented as opposed to the Trilogy way. It is always best to display a spacial parameter in its proper orientation if possible. The conclusion stated in the Trilogy example ignores the fact that the highest temperatures recorded are north of the equator. My 2039 point analysis shows that this is not just anomolous, but a clear pattern. So, the Trilogy analysis is flawed, and the flawed technique did show the pattern, but not as distinctly as my comparison did. Perhaps the more important lesson here is to not let your assumption blind you to the reality of the data.
